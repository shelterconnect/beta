What is ShelterConnect?

We all idealize the idea of donating food. It seems wrong to throw out perfectly good food, so if we have a large enough amount of it (say after a party), we bring it to the local shelter. This seems like an amazing way to give back to the community, but it does have a big flaw. Shelters have a huge logistics problem with donations. The more well known shelters end up with too much food, and if it is perishable, they can't serve all of it. Other shelters end up with too little food, and some people go hungry. The same problem applies with volunteers. Four high school students (Zach Latta, Julian Poyourow, Mateo Abascal, and Ankur Jain) saw this problem, and decided to do something about it. ShelterConnect is like an RSVP system for donations. Restaurants, individuals, schools, and anyone else with surplus food posts how many servings they have, and shelters post how much food and how many volunteers they need. Then, our algorithm does its magic, and tells volunteers where to pick up food, and where to bring it. Now every shelter can get the food and volunteers they need.
